Dog Care 🐶

Helpful guides for healthy and active dogs.

Nutrition and Feeding

A balanced diet is essential for a dog’s growth, energy and immune system. Dogs require protein, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins and minerals in the right amounts.

Choose high-quality dog food or vet-approved diets based on age, breed and activity level. Always provide fresh water.

Exercise and Physical Activity

Regular exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and prevents behavioral issues. Most dogs need daily walks, playtime and mental stimulation.

Active breeds may need longer exercise sessions, while older dogs require gentler activity.

Grooming and Hygiene

  • Regular brushing to remove loose hair and prevent tangles
  • Bathing with dog-safe shampoo when needed
  • Nail trimming to prevent discomfort
  • Ear cleaning to reduce infection risk
  • Dental care to maintain oral health

Health Care and Veterinary Visits

Routine veterinary visits help detect health issues early and keep vaccinations up to date.

  • Vaccinations for diseases like rabies
  • Parasite prevention (fleas, ticks, worms)
  • Regular health check-ups

Common Health Issues

  • Canine Parvovirus – a serious viral infection
  • Hip Dysplasia – a joint condition affecting mobility
  • Obesity – caused by overfeeding and lack of exercise

Training and Behavior

Training helps dogs learn good behavior and ensures safety. Positive reinforcement methods (treats and praise) are most effective.

Early socialization helps dogs become comfortable with people and other animals.

Emotional Well-being

Dogs need attention, companionship, and mental stimulation. Lack of interaction can lead to anxiety or destructive behavior.

Safety and Responsible Ownership

  • Secure your home and yard
  • Use ID tags and microchipping
  • Supervise dogs in unfamiliar environments
  • Respect public spaces and clean up after your pet

When to Visit a Veterinarian

  • Loss of appetite or sudden weight changes
  • Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than a day
  • Difficulty breathing or walking
  • Unusual behavior or signs of pain
