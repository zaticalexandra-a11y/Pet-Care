Creating a Safe Rabbit Habitat 🐰

Rabbits need spacious, clean, and secure environments. Providing hiding spots, soft bedding, and safe toys helps rabbits feel comfortable and relaxed.

Space and Enclosure

Rabbits should have enough space to move freely, stretch, and stand on their hind legs. A large indoor pen or rabbit-proofed room is often better than a small cage.

Bedding and Cleanliness

Use soft, safe bedding such as hay or paper-based materials. The habitat should be cleaned regularly to prevent odor buildup and reduce the risk of infections.

Hiding Spots and Comfort

Rabbits are prey animals and feel safest when they have hiding spaces. Provide boxes, tunnels, or covered areas where they can rest and feel secure.

Safe Toys and Enrichment

Chew-safe toys, tunnels, and cardboard items help keep rabbits mentally stimulated and prevent boredom. Avoid plastic or toxic materials that could be harmful if chewed.

Safety Tips

  • Keep electrical wires and toxic plants out of reach
  • Ensure the enclosure is escape-proof
  • Provide constant access to fresh hay and water
  • Avoid loud noises and stressful environments
