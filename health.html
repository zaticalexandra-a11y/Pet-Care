Pet Health ❤️

Regular vet visits and vaccinations are essential for keeping pets healthy. Monitoring behavior and eating habits can help detect health problems early.

Veterinary Care

Pets should have routine check-ups at least once a year. Puppies, kittens, senior pets, and animals with health conditions may need more frequent visits. Vets monitor weight, dental health, heart function and overall condition.

Vaccinations

Vaccinations protect pets from serious infectious diseases. Dogs are commonly vaccinated against rabies, distemper, and parvovirus, while cats are vaccinated against feline panleukopenia and respiratory diseases. Vaccination schedules depend on age, lifestyle and risk level.

Parasite Prevention

Fleas, ticks, and worms can cause serious health issues if untreated. Regular preventive treatments help protect pets from skin irritation, infections, anemia and internal diseases.

Monitoring Health at Home

  • Changes in appetite or drinking habits
  • Lethargy or unusual tiredness
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, or coughing
  • Sudden weight loss or gain
  • Changes in behavior or activity

When to See a Vet Immediately

  • Difficulty breathing or severe weakness
  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Signs of pain or injury
  • Seizures or collapse
