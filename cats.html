Cat Care 🐱

Everything you need to know about caring for cats.

Nutrition and Feeding

Cats are obligate carnivores, which means they need a diet mainly based on animal protein. A balanced diet supports their energy, coat health and immune system.

Choose high-quality cat food based on age, weight and health condition. Always provide fresh, clean water.

Exercise and Play

Although cats are independent, they still need daily play and stimulation. Interactive toys and climbing structures help keep them active.

Regular play prevents obesity and reduces stress-related behavior.

Grooming and Hygiene

  • Regular brushing to reduce shedding and hairballs
  • Clean litter box daily
  • Nail trimming when needed
  • Occasional bathing (only if necessary)
  • Ear and eye checks for dirt or infection

Health Care and Veterinary Visits

Regular vet check-ups help detect diseases early and ensure vaccinations are up to date.

  • Vaccinations (e.g. feline distemper, rabies)
  • Parasite prevention (fleas, worms)
  • Routine health screenings

Common Health Issues

  • Urinary tract infections
  • Obesity from overfeeding
  • Dental disease
  • Hairballs

Behavior and Communication

Cats communicate through body language, vocal sounds and behavior. Understanding these signals helps build a stronger bond.

Scratching, kneading and purring are natural behaviors not misbehavior.

Emotional Well-being

Cats need a safe environment, routine and mental stimulation. Stress can lead to behavioral or health problems.

Safety and Responsible Ownership

  • Keep toxic plants and foods out of reach
  • Provide safe indoor/outdoor environments
  • Use identification (collar or microchip)
  • Regularly check for injuries or illness

When to Visit a Veterinarian

  • Loss of appetite or sudden weight changes
  • Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than a day
  • Difficulty urinating
  • Behavior changes or signs of pain
