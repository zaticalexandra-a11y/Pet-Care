Small Pets Care 🐹

Care tips for rabbits, hamsters and guinea pigs.

General Care for Small Pets

Small pets such as rabbits, hamsters and guinea pigs need a safe, clean and comfortable environment. Proper care includes the right diet, housing and daily attention.

Nutrition and Feeding

Each small pet has specific dietary needs. Fresh food, hay and clean water are essential.

  • Rabbits: Unlimited hay, fresh vegetables, pellets
  • Hamsters: Seeds, grains, vegetables in small amounts
  • Guinea pigs: Hay, vitamin C-rich vegetables, pellets

Housing and Environment

A proper cage or enclosure is important for safety and comfort.

  • Spacious and well-ventilated cages
  • Soft bedding material
  • Regular cleaning to prevent disease
  • Safe hiding spaces for stress reduction

Exercise and Activity

Small pets need daily movement to stay healthy and happy.

  • Rabbits: Free-roam time or play pens
  • Hamsters: Running wheels and tunnels
  • Guinea pigs: Floor time outside the cage

Grooming and Hygiene

  • Regular brushing (especially for long-haired rabbits)
  • Clean cages at least once a week
  • Nail trimming when needed
  • Check for parasites or skin issues

Health Care and Veterinary Visits

Regular check-ups help prevent common health problems in small pets.

  • Dental care (very important for rabbits and guinea pigs)
  • Parasite prevention
  • Monitoring weight and eating habits

Common Health Issues

  • Overgrown teeth (rabbits & guinea pigs)
  • Respiratory infections
  • Digestive problems
  • Stress-related illnesses

Behavior and Social Needs

Some small pets are social animals and should not be kept alone. Understanding their behavior improves their well-being.

  • Rabbits: Prefer companionship of other rabbits
  • Guinea pigs: Very social, should live in pairs
  • Hamsters: Usually solitary animals

Safety and Responsible Ownership

  • Protect from predators and household dangers
  • Avoid toxic foods and plants
  • Handle gently to avoid stress or injury
  • Provide a quiet and stable environment

When to Visit a Veterinarian

  • Loss of appetite or not eating
  • Diarrhea or digestive issues
  • Breathing problems
  • Unusual behavior or lethargy
